Axolotl (Ambystoma mexicanum)

The axolotl is only native to Lake Xochimilco (pronounced SO-chee-MILL-koh) and Lake Chalco in the Valley of Mexico.
Axolotls are long-lived, surviving up to 15 years on a diet of mollusks, worms, insect larvae, crustaceans, and some fish.

Fun fact:

According to the legend, this “water monster” was a god who disguised himself as a salamander to avoid sacrifice...and is capable of the regeneration of some parts of their bodies!

Tlaloc's leopard Frog (Lithobates tlaloci)

Is an endemic amphibian from Mexico City, which still resides in wetlands.

Fun fact

aztec folklore the croaking of the frogs is associated with the arrival of the rains and the amphibians appear dressed in blue as assistants of Tlaloc, the supreme aztec god of the rain.

The frog of the Pedregal (Eleutherodactylus grandis)

Its only habitat is the volcanic spill of Xitle volcano, current Pedregal de San Ángel.
This frog is smaller than a $ 1 mexico peso coin.

Fun fact:

best thing is that the song of this frog is a whistle. And although it is difficult to find it on a daily basis, those who live near the area can soon know it by sound, very similar to the song of a cricket.
